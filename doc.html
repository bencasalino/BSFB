LEAGUE CONSTITUTION

OVERVIEW

BSFB is a true franchise building experience designed to mimic the NBA as closely as fantasy sports will allow.

Scoring System is Head-to-Head Total Points: Teams compete Head to Head to earn a single Win, Loss or Tie each week based on which team scores the most Fantasy Points in a scoring period. All 30 NBA Franchises and playes will be represented. Full 82 Game Schedule for 12 teams with 8 making the playoffs with realistic year round off-season activity.

Our League began circa 2007-08 in the dorms of Arizona State University in Tempe. (The same dorms that housed future NBA All-Star James Harden). This October we will be entering our 8th season as a league. Below is our "League Constitution", where all league rules and detailes will be contained.

PLAYER LIMIT

Each team can start a max of 40 players each week. Going over that limit before or after the week is over will result in the HIGHEST scoring player(s) from the final day being SUBTRACTED from your total score of the week. It is up to the managers to field exactly their best 40 to avoid the penalty.

If a player is placed in a roster spot in your lineup but does NOT play any minutes, then that player will NOT be added to your team’s weekly total.

* If for some reason ESPN decides to count a player put into your active roster towards your total AND that player did not enter the game at any point, please message a commissioner so we can edit the teams weekly total.


The Weekly Player Total can be viewed by clicking the "Full Box Score" button inside the matchup window on the "scoreboard tab".


There is no automatic block for playing over 40 players from ESPN. It will let you go over the player limit by default, but one of us will check the weekly totals on sunday nights after the completetion of all games and adjust the scores if a manager has gone over the limit. All scoring adjustments are then posted to the leauge page the following day for transparency.





TRADES

Trading Draft Picks:
Managers are allowed to trade draft picks once they are officially signed up for that current season. ALL draft picks must be done prior to the first pick of the draft. (Our leauge currently does not allow trading of future draft picks.)

Trading Players:
Managers are allowed to trade players immediately after the has concluded. Our league trade deadline has always been March 1st (two weeks after the official NBA trade deadline.) Managers are encouraged to post on the leauge Facebook Page with who is on their "trading block" and/or any postion they are looking for specifically in a trade.

TRADE RULES

Once a trade has been accpected by both teams the changes will not take affect for 48 hours(if approved). A league commssioner will post the trade on the league page with voting options. Managers then have 2 days to vote to "accept" or "veto" the trade.

ALL trades must have less than 5 votes from other managers within 2 days after a trade for it to be confirmed.

In most cases trades will be approved. However if the trade is unblanced and provides a substanstial advantage to a team, then it is up to the other owners to veto the trade an maintain league integrity.

WAIVERS

There is No Daily waiver limit or waiver order.

Players can be added to teams at anytime during the season however a player added is not available until the following day.

NO INJURY RESERVE

Our league has no dedicated Injury Reserve spot. Instead all injured players are treated like normal players and remain in a bench slot until available.

DRAFT PICKS

The following seasons draft picks are based on the final standings. (Example 5th place would get the 5th pick in next year's draft.)

PROTECTED PICKS

In order to retain a draft pick from the previous season managers must sign up before the protected draft pick deadline. The deadline is usually August 1st. After that day, any new/old manager can sign up for the leauge.

MISC. RULES


Roster Dumping: When a team is out of playoff contention and drops the best players on their roster. Or on the final day of a matchup (usually in playoffs) a team drops majority of its roster to obtain a win. This can be a rare occurance and can even be done unintentionally but needs to be said that this is extremely frouwned upon to drop a large portion of your roster and obstructing the waiver wire.
