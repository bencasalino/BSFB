LEAGUE CONSTITUTION

OVERVIEW

BSFB is a true franchise building experience designed to mimic the NBA as closely as fantasy sports will allow.

Scoring System is Head-to-Head Total Points: Teams compete Head to Head to earn a single Win, Loss or Tie each week based on which team scores the most Fantasy Points in a scoring period. All 30 NBA Franchises and playes will be represented. Full 82 Game Schedule for 12 teams with 8 making the playoffs with realistic year round off-season activity.

Our League began circa 2007-08 in the dorms of Arizona State University in Tempe. (The same dorms that housed future NBA All-Star James Harden). This October we will be entering our 8th season as a league. Below is our "League Constitution", where all league rules and detailes will be contained.

PLAYER LIMIT

Each team can start a max of 40 players each week (Unless it’s a short/long week) Going over that limit before or after the week is over will result in the HIGHEST scoring player(s) from the final day being SUBTRACTED from your total score of the week. It is up to the managers to field exactly their best 40 to avoid the penalty and keeping the league fair. If a player is placed in a roster spot in your lineup but does NOT play any minutes, then that player will not be added to your team’s weekly total.


The Weekly Player Total can be viewed by clicking the "Full Box Score" button inside the matchup window on the "scoreboard tab".


There is no automatic block for playing over 40 players from ESPN. It will let you go over the player limit by default, but one of us will check the weekly totals on sunday nights after the completetion of all games and adjust the scores if a manager has gone over the limit. All scoring adjustments are then posted to the leauge page the following day for transparency.

WAIVERS

There is No Daily waiver limit or waiver order.

Players can be added to teams at anytime during the season however a player added is not available until the following day.

NO INJURY RESERVE

Our league has no dedicated Injury Reserve spot. Instead all injured players are treated like normal players and remain in a bench slot until available.

MISC. RULES

Defintion

Roster Dumping: When a team is out of playoff contention and drops the best players on their roster. Or on the final day of a matchup (usually in playoffs) a team drops majority of its roster to obtain a win. This can be a rare occurance and can even be done unintentionally but needs to be said that this is extremely frouwned upon to drop a large portion of your roster and obstructing the waiver wire.
